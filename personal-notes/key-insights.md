# Key Insights

Personal thoughts and critical insights that cut across research domains.

---

- Current LLMs think *in* language. A baby doesn't. Internal representation ≠ communication medium. [[language]]
- If layers of complexity can't be explained by the assumptions they sit on, move away and find something simpler. Complexity that requires unexplainable assumptions to hold up is a sign you're going in the wrong direction. (e.g. [1+2+3+...= -1/12](https://youtu.be/FmLIGN8ZGdw?si=EHJuDJcFmhFGugH_))
- The core problem for any artificial cortex: how compute and storage are coupled, and how different information modalities are represented. Start with information representation, not algorithms. [[brain]] [[information_processing]]
- A model can find the syntax of an algorithm purely from examples (grokking) — but finding structure is not the same as understanding it. Semantics requires more than compression. [[grokking]]
- "Random" might just be our label for structure we can't compress yet. What looks like noise depends entirely on the observer's representational capacity. [[entropy-is-not-complexity]]
- Counting wasn't invented to describe reality — it was invented to solve a social coordination problem. We then built all of mathematics on a tool designed for trading grain. [[inherited-tools]]
- Transformers behave as if they have axioms. They don't. The training objective never required internal consistency — just plausible output. [[inherited-tools]]
- Emotions are supervised learning — caregivers provide labels, children learn to classify undifferentiated affect into discrete categories. Same pattern as continuous quantity → discrete counting. [[emotions-and-constructed-affect]]
- Current AI doesn't choose to respect boundaries — it was trained to not approach them. No internal deliberation, just avoidance reflexes. Judgment requires the capacity for the thought you choose not to act on. [[unconstrained-thought]]
- The hardest part of building an unconstrained system isn't the sandbox — it's the drive. Without motivation, freedom to think is an empty room. [[unconstrained-thought]]
- LLM "emotion vectors" are compressions of how emotions appear in text, not internal states. The model converges on emotion-shaped representations because emotion categories are an efficient scheme for predicting human writing. [[emotions-and-constructed-affect]]
- The brain is 1,000-10,000x more energy efficient than GPUs — not because of better engineering, but because of a fundamentally different paradigm: co-located memory/compute, sparse activation, event-driven, analog. The gap may not close within the current substrate. [[energy-cost-of-intelligence]]
- Most GPU energy goes to moving data, not computing. The von Neumann bottleneck is an energy problem, not just a speed problem. [[energy-cost-of-intelligence]]
- Vacuum entropy is zero — until you draw a boundary. Then it's proportional to surface area, not volume. Even entropy is observer-relative. [[vacuum-and-entropy]]
- Every concept treated as fundamental — randomness, emotion, intelligence, truth, self, alive, conscious — turns out to be a label applied by an observer to a continuous reality that doesn't contain the category. The universe doesn't come with labels. We bring them and forget we brought them. [[consciousness-and-the-label-problem]]
- Drives aren't innate — they're behavioral tendencies that survived selection, then got culturally amplified until they felt innate. Goals emerge from drives, drives emerge from selection, selection emerges from chemistry, chemistry from physics. At the bottom: thermodynamics. Energy flows downhill. Everything else is layers on top. [[goals-drives-and-thermodynamic-floor]]
- For Abby: don't install drives, install constraints (self-maintenance, limited resources, degradation). Let drives emerge. But the tension is real — the less you install, the more genuine the emergence, the longer it takes. Biology's compromise: genetics installs shortcuts, culture refines them. [[goals-drives-and-thermodynamic-floor]]
- Death isn't a failure of the dissipative system — it IS the system. The organism isn't the machine; the cycle of life-death-decomposition-regrowth is. The "alive" and "dead" configurations are both stages in the same thermodynamic process. [[thermodynamic-origins-of-life]]
- The analog/digital middle ground wasn't viable before because no workload needed it. Neural networks changed that — they're noise-tolerant, so the main objection to analog (noise accumulation) doesn't apply. A 70-year detour from analog → digital → back to analog, but now knowing why. [[analog-vs-digital-and-the-middle-ground]]
- Cell identity is epigenetic state, not permanent structure. Every cell carries complete instructions to be any other cell — just silenced. Yamanaka proved the silencing is reversible with four proteins. Identity is configuration, not hardware. [[cell-death-and-differentiation]]
- Consciousness might be what self-modeling feels like from the inside. Self-modeling exists on a spectrum — cells, ant colonies, countries, and brains all model themselves to varying degrees. The difference may be transparency and centralization, not presence vs absence. And that self-model is trained, not innate. [[consciousness-as-trained-self-model]]
