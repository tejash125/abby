# Goals as a Learned Representation

## The framework that prompted this (Levin's diverse intelligence)

Strip the examples and the claims are:

1. **Intelligence is a thing you do, not a thing you have.** Working definition (from William James): the ability to reach the same goal by different means. The test is not "has a brain" but "can it still get where it's going when the world changes on it?"
2. **It's a continuum, not a switch.** Don't ask "intelligent: yes/no." Ask "what is it trying to do, over what range of space and time?" That range is the **cognitive light cone** — how far a system's goals reach in space and how far ahead in time. A bacterium's goals are microns wide and minutes long; a human's reach across the planet and decades.
3. **The same coordination trick scales and runs on strange material.** A single cell pursues tiny goals. Glue cells together and the *group* pursues goals no single cell has — "build exactly five fingers, rebuild them if cut off." They coordinate with electrical signals between cells: the same physical mechanism a brain uses, slower, in body-shape-space instead of thought-space.
4. **Living systems are built for novelty, not for a script.** Tadpoles with an eye grafted onto the tail can see — immediately, no evolution, no retraining. A system organized around a goal finds a new route the first time it is perturbed. Growth is a goal-seeker improvising, not DNA cranking through fixed rules.

(Levin's far end — "particles are cognitive," "try to train a hurricane" — is a research provocation, not a result. The cell and tissue work is experimental; the "minimal matter" claim is a bet about where the continuum bottoms out. Keep them separate.)

## The disagreement: is the goal primary?

Levin treats the **setpoint as primary** — the system *is* a goal-homeostat (it defends a target the way a thermostat defends a temperature: knock it off, it pushes back), and that is *why* it generalizes. On this reading, goal-directedness is the architectural primitive and good prediction is downstream of it.

A **setpoint** here means a target a system steers back toward when knocked away from it.

The objection: **a newborn does not arrive with goals.** A human baby, an animal baby — they learn what to steer toward from the environment they land in, and the environment heavily determines how much the goal varies between one entity and the next. If goals were the installed primitive, they would not be this contingent on where you happen to grow up.

## The resolution: a goal is a representation, encoded dynamically

A goal is not a different *kind* of thing from a representation. It **is** a representation — one that points at "what to steer toward" rather than "what is out there." It is:

- **Learned, not installed.** Encoded into the system from experience and from a trusted social environment, the same way meaning gets attached to anything else.
- **Dynamic.** It changes over time as the system's reach grows — i.e. as the cognitive light cone expands. New goals become representable only once the system can model far enough in space and time to hold them.
- **Contingent.** Because it is learned from environment, it varies across entities and across the same entity over its life.

This does not weaken the representation-first bet — it *absorbs* the strongest counter-argument into it. Levin's "the goal-seeker is what generalizes" becomes "a substrate that learns good representations grows goals, and the goals it grows are what we later point at and call generalization." Goal-directedness is the emergent thing; it is not a separate primitive that has to be wired in.

## Why this matters for the project

- **What you install is constraints, not goals.** Goals are the thing that *emerges* on a representational substrate under those constraints — consistent with the earlier decision to install constraints and let drives emerge. See [[goals-drives-and-thermodynamic-floor]].
- **The cognitive light cone is a candidate progress axis.** "Grow the reach of the system's goals in space and time" is one measurable line to track instead of a pile of separate milestones. A system that learns to steer toward states minutes and hours ahead is a light cone widening in time — which is the same direction as understanding time at all.
- **Goals-as-representation is the same shape as other constructed things.** Emotion categories are built by caregivers labeling undifferentiated stimulus ([[emotions-and-constructed-affect]]); the self is a trained model ([[consciousness-as-trained-self-model]]); intelligence is contextual to a learned belief system ([[intelligence]]). Goals join the same list: not innate, encoded from environment, revisable.

## Open question

If goals are learned representations that expand the light cone, what is the *minimal* seed that lets the first goal form at all? A pure pattern-learner with no needs has no reason to steer toward anything. The seed is probably the installed constraints (limited resources, self-maintenance, degradation) plus a trusted source whose signals are treated as salient — but "how little is enough" is unresolved.
