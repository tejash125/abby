# Vacuum and Entropy

## Creating Vacuum

Progressively harder stages of pumping:

1. **Mechanical pumps** — physically push air out. ~1/1000th atmospheric pressure.
2. **Turbomolecular pumps** — high-speed blades knock individual gas molecules out. ~10⁻⁹ atm.
3. **Ion pumps + baking** — ionize and trap remaining molecules, bake chamber walls to release absorbed gas. ~10⁻¹² atm.

Even the best lab vacuum: ~10⁷ molecules/cm³. Room air: ~10¹⁹ molecules/cm³. Intergalactic space: ~1 atom/m³. Perfect vacuum has never been achieved on Earth.

## Vacuum Is Not Empty

Classical view: vacuum = void, absence of everything.

Quantum view: vacuum = the **ground state** of quantum fields that permeate all of space. The fields are still there — just in their lowest energy configuration. And quantum mechanics forbids them from being perfectly quiet.

**Quantum field fluctuations:** The uncertainty principle applied to fields means energy fluctuates even in the ground state. Virtual particle-antiparticle pairs pop in and out of existence continuously.

**Casimir effect:** Two metal plates close together in vacuum experience a measurable attractive force. Between the plates, only field fluctuations with wavelengths that fit can exist. Outside, all wavelengths exist. The imbalance creates net pressure pushing plates together. Experimentally confirmed — "nothing" exerts a measurable force.

**Zero-point energy:** The ground-state fluctuations carry non-zero energy. Vacuum has an energy density.

**Higgs field:** Unlike other fields, the Higgs field has a non-zero value in its ground state. It permeates all of space, including every vacuum chamber ever built.

## The Vacuum Catastrophe

QFT predicts an enormous vacuum energy density. The observed dark energy (accelerating universe expansion) is ~10¹²⁰ times smaller than the prediction. The worst discrepancy between theory and observation in all of physics. Unresolved.

## Entropy of Vacuum

**Classical:** Zero. No particles, no microstates.

**Quantum (whole system):** Zero. The vacuum ground state is a pure state; von Neumann entropy of a pure state is zero.

**Quantum (subregion):** Non-zero. Drawing a boundary creates entanglement entropy — the fields inside the region are entangled with fields outside. Tracing over the outside produces a mixed state with non-zero entropy.

The entropy is proportional to the **surface area of the boundary, not the volume.** More boundary = more entangled pairs across the cut = more entropy. The interior doesn't matter; only the boundary does.

## Black Holes Follow the Same Law

Bekenstein-Hawking entropy: S = kc³A / 4Għ

Black hole entropy is proportional to the area of the event horizon, not the volume inside. Same scaling as vacuum entanglement entropy. Probably not a coincidence — but not fully understood.

This leads to the **holographic principle**: the maximum information content of any region of space is encoded on its boundary, not in its volume.

## Observer-Dependence of Vacuum Entropy

The vacuum has zero entropy — until you draw a boundary. The moment an observer defines "inside" and "outside," entropy appears. It wasn't hiding in the vacuum. It was created by the act of defining a perspective.

Same pattern as: complexity depends on the observer, "random" depends on compression ability, emotion categories depend on conceptual training. Even entropy — the most physical quantity in thermodynamics — is relative to a boundary someone chose to draw.
