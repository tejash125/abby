# Mitochondria and Energy (Picard)

Notes from a long conversation with Dr. Martin Picard, a mitochondrial biologist
at Columbia. Relevant to our prior biology thread ([[thermodynamic-origins-of-life]],
[[cell-death-and-differentiation]], [[selfish-gene-theory]]) and, more sharply, to
Abby's decay-seed: Picard describes real biological signals a body *feels from the
inside* when it is "running out of energy" — which is exactly the kind of built-in,
feelable condition we argued Abby needs.

**Honesty note up front.** This is a pop-science interview, and Picard mixes
solid mechanism (endosymbiosis, ATP, apoptosis, the Warburg effect) with (a) his
own proposed unifying framework ("energy resistance"), which is a hypothesis, not
settled consensus, and (b) some genuinely woo-adjacent framing ("we *are* energy,"
purpose as a laser, "love is resonance"). I separate those below rather than
laundering the loose bits as established fact.

---

## What a mitochondrion actually is and does

A mitochondrion is a tiny structure inside cells (about a thousand per cell, on the
order of thousands of trillions in a body). Its core job: take the electrons bound
up in food and let them *flow*, like current in a circuit, toward the oxygen you
breathe — food-electrons → oxygen → water. That controlled flow is what "being
alive" runs on. As the electrons flow, the mitochondrion charges up like a little
battery and uses that charge to make **ATP** (adenosine triphosphate — the small
molecule cells spend as their energy currency: want a muscle to contract, you spend
ATP). The same flow also throws off **heat** (why bodies are warm) and **signals**.

Mitochondria were once free-living bacteria. ~1.5 billion years ago one cell took
another inside it (endosymbiosis); the guest became the power supply. Picard's
preferred reading of *why that mattered* is the interesting part: the extra energy
did not just let cells get bigger — it let them **perceive their environment and
compute differently**, which (he argues) is what made cells *social* — able to
specialize and divide labor, the root of organs and multicellular bodies. Whether
or not that specific causal story holds, the framing "more energy bought more
computation and cooperation" connects to [[emergence]] and [[information_processing]].

## The energy-first framing (and its caveat)

Picard's headline is "you are the energy flowing through your body, not the body."
As metaphysics that is loose. But there is a defensible core: the difference
between a living person and a fresh cadaver is not the *stuff* (same molecules for a
while) — it is the *ongoing flow and transformation of energy*. A living thing is a
process, not a substance. That is the same move physics makes in
[[foundational-blocks-universe-and-abby]] (process/relation over substance) and the
same one we make for Abby (a learner is a *loop*, not a stored thing).

## A fixed energy budget, allocated by priority

Two claims that matter for Abby:

1. **The budget is roughly fixed.** You cannot simply "add more energy" by eating
   more; overloading the system is *harmful*, not helpful. You can only become more
   *efficient* (e.g. exercise → the body builds more mitochondria during recovery,
   so the same task later costs less).
2. **The budget is allocated by a priority hierarchy** (he analogizes to Maslow's
   hierarchy of needs). Survival-critical demands are paid first; "low-priority"
   maintenance (hair color, skin repair, long-horizon upkeep) is starved when a
   threat is present. Chronic stress reassigns energy away from *growth,
   maintenance, and repair* — which is, on this account, much of what aging *is*.

The vivid demonstration: **hair graying is reversible within a window.** A single
hair records history like tree rings; hairs go gray during high-stress months and
can regain color when stress lifts (vacation, a fasting period), until enough damage
accumulates past a threshold and it is permanent. Gray hair is the *last domino* —
a visible readout of energy being diverted away from low-priority upkeep.

This "finite budget, spent by priority, survival first" picture is a strong outside
parallel to Abby's **decay seed**: a single scarce internal quantity whose
allocation *is* the pressure that creates goals, relevance, and urgency. See
[[goals-drives-and-thermodynamic-floor]] and [[goals-as-learned-representation]].

## "Energy resistance" — Picard's framework (flagged as hypothesis)

His proposed unifying principle: **resistance = energy demand ÷ flow capacity.**
When demand outruns the mitochondria's ability to flow energy, "resistance" rises,
the system overheats (inflammation, oxidative stress), and things break. He maps
major diseases onto this:

- **Diabetes / insulin resistance** — too much glucose pushed onto cells; the cell
  *protects itself* by refusing insulin (pulling in its receptors). Obesity, on
  this read, is *also* a protection mechanism — storing excess energy away from
  delicate tissue.
- **Cancer** — the **Warburg effect**: cancer cells "ditch" their mitochondria and
  revert to an ancestral, oxygen-independent, selfish mode, replicating for
  themselves and escaping the mitochondria's *veto over cell death* (apoptosis — the
  normal, orderly self-destruct a defective cell is supposed to run for the good of
  the whole). Cancer as a cell *defecting from the social contract* of the body.
- **Alzheimer's** — reframed as an energy disorder ("type 3 diabetes"): affected
  regions first go *hyper*-metabolic (burning more, straining to compensate — like
  the graying follicle), then *hypo*-metabolic (burning less, when symptoms appear).
  He is skeptical of the amyloid-plaque-as-cause story (people with heavy plaques and
  normal cognition exist, and vice versa).

Treat "energy resistance unifies all disease" as *his lens*, not textbook. But the
cancer-as-defector framing is a clean echo of [[selfish-gene-theory]] and
[[cell-death-and-differentiation]]: a multicellular body is a truce among cells, and
cancer is that truce breaking.

## Mitochondria as a signaling network — an "intracellular brain"

Mitochondria do far more than make ATP. Their surfaces carry receptors; they sense
conditions inside and outside the cell (is there enough energy? a stress hormone? a
threat?), talk to each other, and coordinate — Picard calls them a "distributed
intracellular brain," and describes cross-organ signaling (liver mitochondria make
ketones that feed brain mitochondria). This is another instance of the recurring
Abby theme: **cognition-like behavior as a network of many small units exchanging
signals**, not a central controller. See [[brain]] and [[emergence]].

## The part most relevant to Abby: energy signals you *feel from the inside*

This is the gold. The body has real molecular signals that report "something is
running low on energy," and those signals *change behavior and motivation* — exactly
the "feelable from the inside" condition we said Abby's decay must be:

- **GDF15** — a protein made by nearly every organ *except* the brain, whose
  receptor sits *only* in the brainstem's nausea/aversion center. When energetic
  stress is high, GDF15 rises, docks in the brainstem, and the brain reads it as
  "something in the body is running out of energy." Its two responses: **conserve**
  (sickness behavior — lose motivation, withdraw, feel depressed, "don't feel like
  it's worth it") and **mobilize** (dump glucose and fat into the blood to rescue
  starving cells). Chronically high GDF15 predicts (UK Biobank, ~14-year follow-up)
  mental illness, cardiovascular disease, and death. It is, literally, an internal
  low-fuel gauge that reshapes what you want to do.
- **Lactate / CO2** — signals that mitochondria "can't keep up." We evolved to feel
  them acutely; injecting lactate can *trigger a panic attack*, and in people with
  traumatic memories can reawaken them. A raw internal energy-deficit signal wired
  straight to alarm.
- **Sickness behavior** generally — the withdrawal, aversion to effort, loss of
  caring when ill — is an *energy-conservation strategy*, not a malfunction. ("I was
  still me, but I couldn't care about anything; I was just trying to survive.")

For Abby this is close to a proof-of-concept from biology that our design instinct
is right: a single internal *felt* energy signal, wired to aversion and to a shift
in what the organism will spend effort on, is exactly how real organisms turn a
metabolic fact into motivation. This is [[emotions-and-constructed-affect]]
(interoception — sensing your own internal state) doing real work, and it is the
mechanism the decay-seed is meant to instantiate.

## Two smaller ideas worth keeping

- **We perceive flow/coherence, not amount.** People on intermittent fasting *feel*
  more energetic while eating *fewer* calories — because energy flows more smoothly,
  not because there is more of it. Picard's analogy: a laser and a dim bulb can emit
  the same energy; the laser is powerful because it is *coherent*. He stretches this
  to "purpose focuses energy like a laser" (that part is metaphor, not measurement),
  but the core — *what is felt is the pattern of flow, not the quantity* — is a
  useful reframe, and rhymes with our "Abby's currency is relations/flow, not stored
  amounts."
- **Quality control (mitophagy).** Cells continuously degrade worn-out mitochondria
  and build fresh ones; fasting/exertion accelerates the cull. Maintenance is not a
  one-time build but an ongoing tear-down-and-rebuild — the same "the past lives in
  what is currently maintained" flavor as memory-as-a-web.

## Why this matters for Abby (summary)

1. **Biological backing for the decay-seed.** Real organisms run on a *finite,
   priority-allocated energy budget*; scarcity, not abundance, is what creates
   urgency and shapes behavior. Survival is paid first; everything else is starved
   under stress.
2. **Felt-from-inside energy signals are real and they drive motivation** (GDF15,
   lactate). This is exactly the "must be feelable from the inside or it's
   unlearnable" constraint we set for Abby's decay — and biology implements it as a
   low-fuel signal wired to aversion and to a change in what the organism will do.
3. **A living thing is a process, not stored stuff** — reinforces treating Abby as a
   maintained loop, not a memory box.
4. **Cognition-like coordination emerges from many small signaling units**
   (mitochondria as a network), not a central controller — the recurring
   many-tiny-units theme.
5. **Cancer = a cell defecting from the collective** — a concrete picture of
   multicellular life as a truce, relevant to how "cooperation" and "self vs
   not-self" boundaries could arise, not be installed.
