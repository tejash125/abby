# Phenomena That Defied Physics

Real, measured, reproducible phenomena that violated the physics understood at the time. Some still not fully explained.

## Superfluidity

Helium-4 cooled below 2.17K. Zero viscosity. Behavior:
- Flows through molecule-thin channels with no friction
- Climbs up and over container walls by itself as a thin film, defying gravity
- If the container spins, the fluid stays still
- If set rotating, rotates forever — no friction

Real. Filmed. Reproducible. Violated everything classical physics predicted about fluids.

## Superconductivity

Certain materials below a critical temperature: electrical resistance drops to exactly zero. Not approximately — mathematically zero.
- Current in a superconducting loop flows forever with no energy input (experiments run for years, no decay)
- Magnetic levitation — magnet floats above superconductor indefinitely, no power
- Violated classical understanding that all conductors have some resistance

## Quantum Tunneling

Particle hits a barrier it lacks energy to cross. Classical: bounces back, 100%. Quantum: sometimes appears on the other side. Through the barrier, not over or around.

The Sun burns because of this. Protons don't have enough energy to overcome mutual repulsion. They tunnel through. The Sun shouldn't burn according to classical physics.

Every atom in your hand has a non-zero probability of tunneling through a wall (~10^(-10^30)). Effectively zero, but not actually zero.

## Sonoluminescence

Sound waves in water. Tiny bubbles form, expand, collapse. At collapse: flash of light, interior reaches ~20,000K — hotter than the Sun's surface.

Sound → light. In a glass of water. Room temperature. Not fully explained.

## Quantum Zeno Effect

Observe a quantum system frequently enough and it freezes. Cannot evolve. A radioactive atom that should decay won't decay if measured often enough.

"A watched pot never boils" — literally true in quantum mechanics. Experimentally confirmed.

## Delayed Choice Quantum Eraser

In a double-slit experiment, choosing to measure which slit a photon passed through **after** it already hit the detector appears to retroactively determine wave-vs-particle behavior in the past.

Not actually backwards causation (math resolves carefully), but experimental results are real and look like the future influencing the past.

## Currently Observed, Not Fully Explained

**Dark matter and dark energy:** Our best physics accounts for ~5% of the universe. ~27% is dark matter (gravitational effects, can't see it, don't know what it is). ~68% is dark energy (accelerating expansion, can't explain it). "Dark" means "we have no idea."

**The flyby anomaly:** Spacecraft doing gravitational slingshots around Earth gain slightly more or less energy than general relativity predicts. Multiple missions. Still unexplained.

**The Mpemba effect:** Hot water can freeze faster than cold water under certain conditions. Reported since Aristotle. Reproduced experimentally. Seems to violate basic thermodynamics. No accepted explanation.

**Ball lightning:** Luminous spheres during thunderstorms. Float through rooms, pass through walls, last several seconds. Occasionally photographed. No accepted physical explanation. Not reproducible in labs.

## The Pattern

None of these are "someone defied physics." They are cases where nature defied our understanding of physics. The universe did something, and our model couldn't account for it. Every time:

1. Physics updated to accommodate it (quantum mechanics, relativity)
2. We're still waiting for the update (dark matter, dark energy, flyby anomaly)

Nature doesn't follow our laws. We write laws that try to follow nature. When they diverge, nature is right and the laws get rewritten.

If 95% of the universe is stuff we can't explain, how confident should we be that the 5% we do explain is explained correctly — versus being another approximation that works within conditions we've tested?
